Icelandic Puffin

Puffins are considered to be rare and elusive birds in the majority of the world, but in Iceland, there is a wealth of them. Their arrival in April and May mark the beginning of summer, and they can be easily seen up close in many parts of the country until August.

Top Spots to Watch Puffins in Iceland

  • Dyrhólaey
  • Heimaey island
  • Ingólfshöfði
  • Papey island
  • Borgarfjörður Eystri
  • Grímsey island
  • Látrabjarg cliff

By walking along the edge of this cliff

It is possible to get within arm’s length of the nesting puffins. They have no fear of people, and will only fly off if someone is trying to touch them. At such proximity, the details of their painted beaks and adorable expression are on clear display.
