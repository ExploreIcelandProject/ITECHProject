Eyjafjallajökull Glacier

Although slightly smaller in size, this glacier is more famous than its neighbour, Myrdalsjökull, due to the 2010 eruption of the Eyjafjallajökull volcano. The hiking trail is a fantastic way to see two glaciers in Iceland in one, and it takes you to the very top of the newly erupted volcano as well as the Skógafoss waterfall.Eyjafjallajokull and neighbouring Myrdalsjokull dominate the landscape in South-Iceland and can be seen miles away. We recommend that you make a stop at viewpoints and admire the volcanoes from afar. There are a few along the road.

How To Get There

Driving along the South Shore brings you close to Eyjafjallajokull. The Mountain Range is visible as soon as you drive east following road 1 from Reykjavik and you pass Hellisheidi heath. There you can find Hellisheidi Geothermal Power Plant, which is visible from the road.
